    Following Barbara Windsor death on December 10, 2020, Barbara Windsor’s husband, Scott Mitchell, got £1 million from her television revenue company. Her better partner Mitchell became the sole director of her firm Sauce E Limited the year following her death, according to the formal documents. Mitchell was a 25% owner in the company prior to her death. Until 2019, the company’s accounts showed a working capital of £1 million, including £920,000 in cash. Windsor’s fortune was allegedly amassed while working on the long-running TV series EastEnders. Between 1994 through 2016, she played the role of Peggy Mitchell on the program. Windsor was reputedly in debt to the tune of £1 million prior to her success on the famed television show. However, at the time of her death, the actress’s net worth was reported to be approximately £10 million.

    Barbara Windsor’s Death Resulted in a Deluge of Tributes

    Six years before her death, Windsor was diagnosed with Alzheimer’s disease. Her devoted hubby cared for her over those years, until she was ultimately placed in a London care facility in the summer of 2020. Mitchell issued a statement after her death, stating, “It is with extreme regret that I can report that my lovely wife Barbara [Windsor] died at 8:35 p.m. on Thursday, December 10, at a London care facility.” The spouse said that he spent a week at her side before she died gently as a result of her sickness.

    When the news of Windsor’s death became public, condolences from all over the globe began to pour in on different social media sites. Among the numerous responses was a poignant tribute sent by her EastEnders co-star Patsy Palmer. Following Windsor’s death, actress Palmer turned to Twitter to write a series of messages. “We were like a family for a long time, ups and downs, ins and outs, but you will never meet a more professional actress than Barbs [Windsor],” she tweeted. Similarly, Palmer praised Mitchell’s assistance to his ailing wife, referring to him as the finest better half she could have dreamed for. “You deserve the greatest honor,” the actress said in the last line. In her reminiscence, she said that she will treasure all of their “wonderful experiences” as co-actors. Furthermore, Windsor’s former co-star claimed that she could still hear her laugh and yearned for it to echo in her ears forever.

    Barbara Windsor’s Relationship History with Husband

    Windsor and her husband Mitchell initially met when they were fifty-five and thirty, respectively, after mutual acquaintances introduced them. Windsor was still married to her second husband, Stephen Hollings, at the time she began dating Mitchell. “We were both aware that strong emotions were churning. When I did anything out of character, I had a feeling it was serious “The late actress was quoted as saying. Windsor was the one who initiated their connection by inviting Mitchell on a date.

    Her second husband, upon learning of her affair, mocked her for marrying a guy twenty-five years her junior. In 2000, the pair exchanged vows in a tiny covert ceremony at a Dorchester hotel in the presence of three witnesses. Windsor is survived alone by her third husband since she has no children from any of her previous partnerships.
